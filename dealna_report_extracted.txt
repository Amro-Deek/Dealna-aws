Abstract
Dealnais a secure mobile marketplace application designed exclusively for university
communities, enabling students to buy, sell, and exchange items such as books, electron-
ics, and daily student needs. The platform establishes a trusted environment through
university email verification, role-based access control, and end-to-end encrypted mes-
saging for secure communication. Users may choose to interact using either their real
name or a nickname, providing privacy while maintaining accountability.
The system incorporates a content-based recommendation mechanism to suggest rele-
vantlistingsbasedonuserinterestsandbrowsingbehavior, aswellasaqueue-basedmodel
that ensures fairness in Giveaway transactions. To enhance trust and reduce misuse, the
platform applies moderation policies, rating mechanisms, and fraud-prevention controls.
In addition to student users, Dealna supports verified external service providers through
an administrative approval process, allowing trusted non-student services to participate
in a controlled manner.
The application is designed as a university-scoped system, initially targetingBirzeit
University (BZU)as a pilot deployment. Its architecture allows future expansion to
multiple universities, each operating as an isolated and secure community. The project
evaluation includes a comparative analysis with existing solutions, highlighting Dealna’s
advantages in security, trust, and university-specific access control.

Chapter 1
Introduction
1.1 General overview
Dealnais a marketplace designed to solve real challenges faced by university commu-
nities whenbuying and selling items. Current student marketplaces rely heavily on
social media groups, which suffer from clutter, lack of user verification, and weak security
controls.Dealnaintroduces a structured and trusted environment built on university
email authentication, encrypted communication, and intelligent mechanisms for recom-
mendations and fraud detection.
The platform ensures fairness, trust, and an improved user experience through fea-
tures such as queue-based reservation, Role - based access control, and anonymous post-
ing.Dealnaserves as a modern solution for campus trading, starting withBirzeit
Universityand designed for future expansion across other universities.
1.2 Motivation
University students face several challenges when attempting to buy or sell used items on
campus. Traditional platforms like social media groups are overcrowded with irrelevant
posts and lack identity verification, allowing non students or scammers to infiltrate these
communities. There are no proper filters to help users find what they need, no rating
system to build trust, and no fair method for managing item reservations for Giveaways.
As a result, students often experience confusion, wasted time, and security risks when
using these platforms. There is a clear need for a secure, student focused marketplace
that ensures verification, fairness, and trust while improving the overall experience of
campus-based trading.

2.1 Details of relevant theory
2.1.1 Algorithms
1.Recommendation and Fraud Detection Algorithms
•The system applies a unified TF-IDF withcosine similaritymodel to power
bothrecommendationandfraud detection. Each item’s title and descrip-
tion are transformed into TF-IDF vectors, emphasizing distinctive words while
reducing the weight of common terms [1], [2].
•Cosine Similarity for Text Comparison:Measures the angle between TF-
IDF vectors to assess item similarity, making it scale-invariant and effective
for both short and long descriptions. Huang (2008) demonstrated that cosine
similarity outperforms Jaccard, Euclidean, and Manhattan distance for sparse,
high-dimensional text data [3], [4].
•Forrecommendation, items with high cosine similarity to a user’s viewed or
liked items are suggested (personalized discovery).
•Forfraud detection, items that exceed a predefined similarity threshold with
existing ones are flagged as potential duplicates or spam. This approach en-
sures bothpersonalized discoveryandcontent integrity.
•Statistical Anomaly Detection:Price anomalies are detected using statis-
tical thresholds (mean±3 standard deviations per category). This method
flags abnormally high or low prices that may indicate fraud or pricing errors,
triggering automatic review [9].
•Behavioral Monitoring:Tracks user posting frequency and activity pat-
terns to identify spam or misuse. Detected violations are logged and used to
triggerautomatedrestrictionsormoderationactions, ensuringsystemintegrity
and fair usage .

2.2 Review of past/reported work
2.2.1 Related Work
There are many platforms and apps designed for university students that allow them to
easily buy, sell, and exchange goods, such as UniExchange, Yupa, and Daleel Al Talib (an
Arabic app) [16], [17], [18]. While Daleel Al Talib aims to facilitate exchanges between
students, like other apps, it is limited to basic services like items, posting ads, and user
communication. Most of these apps also lack a robust university verification system,
advanced access control mechanisms, fairness in product exchange and reservation such
as a queue/hold system, reliable user rating system, and secure messaging.
Dealna is the first Arabic application created especially for university students. It offers
a closed and secure environment that relies on university email verification to make sure
every user is a real student. For the current stage, Dealna will focus only on Birzeit
University (BZU), providing a safe space for students to buy, sell, or exchange items
within their own verified community. In the future, the app can grow to include other
universities, where each one will have its own separate and secure community.
The application includes role-based access control to manage user permissions and a
reservation and waiting list feature that help keep exchanges fair for everyone. Students
can browse items easily using filters, post and manage their ads, and the app includes a
rating system based on user feedback after each transaction to ensure a reliable reputation
that fosters trust within the university community.
To maintain security, the app includes a dynamic mechanism that automatically ad-
justs user permissions depending on the number of complaints they receive. In addition,
the system enforces secure input validation to prevent injection attacks, applies session
management controls such as session timeouts and single active sessions.
Another key feature is the option to assign trusted users with special permissions through
the Admin Panel. These users can manage certain types of posts, like those about
university housing, to increase reliability and safety within the platform.
Dealna also uses an intelligent recommendation system that suggests suitable items for
each student based on what they have previously searched for or chosen, making the
experience more personalized and efficient [1], [2], [3], [4].
Finally, DealnaensuresprivacythroughrobustconversationprotectionbasedonSSL/TLS
encryption in transit and end-to-end encryption (E2EE), keeping all messages and attach-
ments confidential and guaranteeing that no third party, including the server, can access
their content. This level of protection is not mentioned in similar applications.
After comparing the features of the other applications mentioned with Dealna, it be-

2.2.2 Applications Review
UniExchange Application
UniExchange is an application designed specifically for university students, functioning
as a closed on-campus marketplace where students can buy, sell, and exchange products
within their university community. The platform requires university email verification
to ensure that all users are real students and includes a rating system to enhance trust
after each transaction. It enforces fair pricing, typically requiring listed items to be
priced between 50–60% of their original value to maintain fairness among students. All
exchanges occur locally within the university, and users can report problematic buyers,
sellers, or messages to maintain a safe environment. Overall, UniExchange offers a simple
and convenient student-to-student exchange experience, with its core features focused on
verification, basic safety, and ease of use.[17]

YUPA Application
YUPA is an application aimed at university students, offering a service-based market-

addresses depending on its data-handling policies. While YUPA offers a flexible and
convenient environment for student services, it does not use university email verification
and operates as an open marketplace rather than a closed, security restricted student
platform.[16]

3.1 Product Description
Dealna is a university-oriented marketplace platform designed to enable safe and regu-

list items, participate in giveaways, complete transactions, communicate securely, and
submit reports, all within a controlled academic environment. User authenticity is en-
sured through university email verification and role-based access control.
The platform supports multiple user roles, including verified students, limited stu-
dents, verified service providers, and administrators. Each role is associated with a spe-
cific set of permissions that determines accessible features. Administrative users interact
withthesystemthroughadedicatedweb-basedadminpanel, whilestudentsandproviders
primarily use the mobile application.

Chapter 4
Conclusion and Future Works
Dealna addresses several limitations found in existing university marketplace platforms
by providing a secure and controlled environment tailored specifically for students and
verified service providers. By relying on university email verification, role-based access
control, and structured transaction and reporting mechanisms, the system promotes trust
and accountability among its users. Features such as queue-based giveaways, controlled
rating aggregation, and secure one-to-one messaging further enhance the reliability of the
platform. The proposed pilot deployment at Birzeit University offers an opportunity to
validate the system design and evaluate its effectiveness in a real academic environment.
4.1 Review of the Project
This project focused on the design and implementation ofDealna, a university-oriented
marketplace intended to support safe and regulated trading within the academic com-
munity. Unlike general-purpose marketplace applications, Dealna emphasizes identity
verification and controlled access through distinct user roles, including verified students,
limited students, verified service providers, and administrators.
The system architecture was designed with a clear separation of concerns between
the backend and frontend components. The backend, implemented using Golang, is
responsible for enforcing business logic, authentication, authorization, transaction rules,
and database operations. In contrast, the Flutter mobile application focuses on user
interaction, interface-level validation, and presenting data received from the backend
without embedding core business rules.
Core functionalities such as user registration, login, item listing management, give-
away queues, transactions, ratings, reporting, and secure messaging were implemented
based ona structureddatabase schema and well-defined RESTfulAPIs. Additional atten-
tion was given to trust-related mechanisms, including rating stability, transaction abuse
prevention, and administrative oversight. Overall, the project achieved its main objec-
tives and demonstrates a practical application of full-stack development concepts within
an academic context.

•Less effective when user behavior changes over time.
Although this method improves over the simple average, it still lacks a principled
probabilistic foundation [22].

.3 Transaction Gaming Prevention and Anti-Fraud
Logic
Online reputation systems are susceptible to manipulation through coordinated or repet-
itive transactions between the same users. Such behavior may be exploited to artifi-
cially inflate reputation scores without representing genuine marketplace activity. This
appendix outlines the threshold-based and history-aware mechanisms employed by the
system to prevent transaction gaming and preserve rating integrity.
•Pairwise Transaction History Monitoring.The system maintains a historical
interaction record for each unique buyer–seller pair. This record includes completed
transactions, associated ratings, transaction timestamps, and rating values. Histor-
ical data is evaluated over rolling time windows to distinguish legitimate repeated
interactions from potentially manipulative behavior.
•Baseline Threshold Definition.A baseline threshold of approximately five com-
pleted transactions between the same pair of users within a rolling 30-day window
is applied. This value is selected to balance the detection of coordinated rating ma-
nipulation while allowing legitimate repeated interactions. Transactions and ratings

that exceed this threshold are considered potentially suspicious and are subject to
further evaluation.
•Adaptive Threshold Adjustment.Threshold values are not strictly fixed and
may be adaptively adjusted based on historical interaction patterns, transaction
timing, and rating behavior. For example, transactions that are evenly distributed
over time or exhibit diverse rating values may be treated as less suspicious than
rapid, repetitive transactions with consistently maximum ratings.
•Suspicious Pattern Detection.When the number of completed transactions
between a user pair exceeds the defined threshold, the system evaluates additional
indicators, including transaction frequency, rating symmetry, and rating extremity.
If suspicious patterns persist, subsequent ratings may be flagged and excluded from
public rating calculations.
•Administrative Notification.Exceeding predefined thresholds automatically
triggers an administrative notification. Administrators are provided with relevant
contextual information, including transaction history, rating timelines, and thresh-
old exceedance details, to support informed review decisions.
•TemporaryRatingFreeze.Incasesofelevatedsuspicion, affectedratingsmaybe
temporarily frozen. Frozen ratings are not included in reputation calculations until
an administrative review is completed, preventing immediate exploitation while
preserving the possibility of reinstatement.
•Administrative Review and Enforcement.Administrators may review flagged
cases and apply appropriate actions, including approving ratings, permanently ex-
cluding them from calculations, issuing warnings, or applying account-level restric-
tions. This layered approach combines automated detection with human oversight
to ensure fairness, accuracy, and system integrity.
Overall, the proposed mechanism provides a balanced and scalable approach to trans-
action gaming prevention, minimizing false positives while effectively protecting the cred-
ibility of the system’s reputation framework.
